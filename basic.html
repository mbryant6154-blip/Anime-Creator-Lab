Basics of Anime Drawing

Anime Drawing Basics & Essential Drawing Tools

Here you will learn the common tools and foundational skills used in anime drawing and their purpose:

Foundational Skills

All anime characters start with simple shapes like circles, ovals, rectangles, and lines. These shapes help build the head, body, arms, and legs before adding details. Once you get advanced and understand the shapes in action poses, the simple shapes become 3D forms like spheres, cubes, and cylinders.

Line art is the clean final outline of a drawing. Line control and line weight reflect confident strokes in your sketch lines, defining which parts will be part of the final line work. For both traditional and digital art, this matters a lot.

Shading adds depth and makes characters look more realistic. Anime shading is often simple; using clear shadow areas instead of complex gradients shows a level of light source direction. Lighting and shading help show where the light source comes from in the scene or add details to the face or clothing.

Consistency and repetition in sketching with a pencil are important. It helps you see proportion and features with your light sketch before the clean and final lines. Learning this Consistency with a pencil can lead you to understanding digital art more.

Digital drawing software can help refine your basic skills. As well as give you the freedom to move between layers, focusing on certain sections of your drawing. Comes with customizing brushes and a color wheel.

Drawing Tools
